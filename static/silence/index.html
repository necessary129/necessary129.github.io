An Open Address to the Student Body 27 March 2026 · IIIT Hyderabad

Who Does Your Silence Protect?

A conversation about the community we're building by doing nothing

When the arbitrary changes to mess policy were being made, there were a few angry emails. When the disciplinary committee ran inquisitions instead of hearings, someone wrote a zinger on the mailing list and we all moved on. Every time, the cycle repeats — all for things we should have gotten in the first place.

Why should we fight to be represented in the spaces we live in? That question is not rhetorical. It is the question this campus has answered, repeatedly, by choosing not to fight at all. A policy drops. A few emails circulate. Someone posts. And we comply. The structure is always the same: decisions made without consultation, no transparency, no accountability, and a population trained to absorb it — to post, to vent, to shrug.

That lesson scales. The habits we build here do not stay within these walls. The instinct to accept what is handed to us without demanding a say does not vanish at convocation. It graduates with us.

  • Parliament passed the Transgender Persons Amendment Bill — stripping the right to self-identify and handing it to a medical board
  • ECI released voter roll revisions that may have removed crores of names from electoral lists across 12 states
  • NCERT curriculum revisions implemented without public consultation or expert review
  • Three-hour social media takedown rule imposed with no legislative debate
  • Students and journalists remain in prison under UAPA without trial — some for over five years

The structure is identical. On campus, the cost is a worse mess and an opaque disciplinary process. Out there, the cost is people's homes, votes, identities, and freedom. The only difference is scale.

Your skills, your taxes, your caste position, and your silence are already deployed. The only question is whether you chose that, or it was chosen for you.

If you have ever said "I'm here to study, not do politics" — this conversation is specifically for you. Because the distinction you are drawing does not exist. There is no neutral ground. Inaction is a position. Comfort is a position. And both of them protect someone. The question is who.

We are not good at fighting for ourselves here. That fact is worth examining — not as an accusation, but as a starting point. Because if we cannot demand transparency from a mess committee, we will not demand it from a parliament.
