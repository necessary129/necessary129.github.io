Who Does Your Silence Protect? A Conversation About the Community We're Building by Doing Nothing

Read · Reflect · Show Up

When the arbitrary changes to mess policy were being made—and continue to be made—there were a few angry emails. When the disciplinary committee ran inquisitions instead of hearings, someone wrote a zinger on the mailing list and we all moved on. When single-use plastics were banned with no consultation, there were a few more. Every time, the cycle repeats, all for things we should have gotten in the first place.

Why should we have to fight to be represented in the spaces we live?

That lesson scales.

In the Last Two Weeks

Beyond the Campus Gate

  • Parliament passed the Transgender Persons Amendment Bill—stripping the right to self-identify and handing it to a medical board.
  • The ECI released a voter roll revision that may have removed crores of names from electoral lists across 12 states.
  • Students and journalists remain in prison under UAPA without trial—some for over five years.
The Pattern

The Structure Is Identical

Decisions made without consultation. No transparency. No accountability. And a population trained to absorb it—to post, to vent, to shrug, and to comply. The only difference is scale.

On Campus

A worse mess.

An opaque disciplinary process.

Policy handed down without consultation.

Out There

People's homes.

Their votes.

Their identities and their freedom.

We're not good at fighting for ourselves here. That's worth examining—because the habit of accepting what's handed to us without demanding a say doesn't stay on campus. It graduates with us.

What This Is

Not a Lecture

This is not a panel of experts telling you what to think. This is a room where we look at what's actually happening—on this campus and in this country—and ask a simple question: who benefits when we look away?

We'll walk through what's been passed and implemented this year, who it targets, how these seemingly separate policies connect, and what people on a campus like ours can actually do about it—starting with the institutions we're already inside.

You don't need to have read anything. You don't need to call yourself political. You just need to show up.

If you've ever said “I'm here to study, not do politics”—this conversation is specifically for you. Because your skills, your taxes, your caste position, and your silence are already deployed. The only question is whether you chose that, or it was chosen for you.
