Open Letter 27 March 2026

Who Does Your Silence Protect?

If you've ever said "I'm here to study, not do politics" — this conversation is specifically for you.

When the arbitrary changes to mess policy were being made, there were a few angry emails. When the disciplinary committee ran inquisitions instead of hearings, someone wrote a zinger on the mailing list and we all moved on. When single-use plastics were banned with no consultation, there were a few more.

Every time, the cycle repeats — all for just things that we should have gotten in the first place. Why should we fight to be represented in the spaces we live in?

That lesson scales.
In the past two weeks
  • The Parliament passed the Transgender Persons Amendment Bill — curbing the right to self-identify by subjecting legal recognition to medical and bureaucratic approval.
  • The Election Commission initiated voter roll revisions across multiple states, affecting crores of voters.
  • The Chief Justice of India directed NCERT to withdraw a social science textbook that referenced facts on judicial corruption.
  • New IT rules enforcing a 3-hour deadline for social media platforms to remove "unlawful content" have come into effect.
  • Sonam Wangchuk was released after detention by Delhi Police during a protest march, with critics arguing the detention itself lacked legal basis.
  • Students, activists, and journalists continue to remain incarcerated under UAPA without trial — in several cases for over five years.

The structure is identical. Decisions made without consultation. No transparency. No accountability. And a population trained to cope — denial, anger, bargaining, depression, then finally, as always, acceptance.

The only difference is scale. On campus, the cost is a worse mess and an opaque disciplinary process. Out there, the cost is people's homes, votes, identities, and freedom.

We're not good at fighting for ourselves here. That's worth examining.

The habit of accepting what's handed to us without demanding a say doesn't stay on campus. It graduates with us.

This is not a lecture. There's no panel of experts telling you what to think. This is a room where we look at what's actually happening — on this campus and in this country — and ask a simple question: who benefits when we look away?

You don't need to have read anything. You don't need to call yourself political. You just need to show up.

Because your skills, your taxes, your caste position, and your silence are already deployed. The only question is whether you chose that, or it was chosen for you.
