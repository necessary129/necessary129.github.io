Who Does Your Silence Protect?

A Conversation About the Community We're Building by Doing Nothing

If you've ever said "I'm here to study, not do politics" — this conversation is specifically for you.

When the arbitrary changes to mess policy were being made — and continue to be made — there were a few angry emails. When the disciplinary committee ran inquisitions instead of hearings, someone wrote a zinger on the mailing list and we all moved on. When single-use plastics were banned with no consultation, there were a few more. Every time, the cycle repeats, all for things we should have gotten in the first place. Why should we fight to be represented in the spaces we live in?

That lesson scales.

"The habit of accepting what's handed to us without demanding a say doesn't stay on campus. It graduates with us."

The structure is identical. Decisions made without consultation. No transparency. No accountability. And a population trained to cope — with denial, anger, bargaining, depression, then finally, as always, acceptance. The only difference is scale. On campus, the cost is a worse mess and an opaque disciplinary process. Out there, the cost is people's homes, votes, identities, and freedom.

It's worth examining this apathy: the habit of accepting what's handed to us without demanding a say doesn't stay on campus. It graduates with us.

  • The Election Commission initiated voter roll revisions across multiple states, affecting crores of voters.

  • New IT rules enforcing a 3-hour deadline for social media platforms to remove "unlawful content" have come into effect.
  • Sonam Wangchuk was released after detention by Delhi Police during a protest march, with critics arguing the detention itself lacked legal basis.
  • Students, activists, and journalists continue to remain incarcerated under UAPA without trial — in several cases for over five years.

"Who Does Your Silence Protect?"

A Conversation About the Community We're Building by Doing Nothing

Date 27th March
Time 6:00 PM
Venue Vindhya A3 117

Open to all

This isn't going to be a lecture or a panel discussion. We want you to think and speak about the state. This is a space where we, together, will look critically at what's actually happening — on this campus and in this country — and ask a simple question: who benefits when we look away?

You don't need to have read anything. You don't need to call yourself political. You just need to show up. Because your skills, your taxes, your caste position, and your silence are already deployed. The only question is whether you chose that, or it was chosen for you.

Hope to see you there.
